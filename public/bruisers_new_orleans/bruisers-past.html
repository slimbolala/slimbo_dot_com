Ghosts of Bruisers Past

Bruisers New Orleans has existed in one form or another since the late 90s. During those years there have been a number of line-up permutations, mainly in the drumming department. (How very "Spinal Tap".) But once a Bruiser (N.O.), always a Bruiser (N.O.). Here's a rundown of the Bruisers past who, though they may have drifted off to greener pastures, are still dear to our hearts. (Sorry, no seething schisms and dramatic splits. Our VH1 "Behind the Music" will be very boring). In chronological-ish order:

Ana Arrien

Ana was a founding member and our drummer for many years during the Bruiser "Classic" line-up (a.k.a. the "Four Non-Blondes" era). Eventually, other obligations compelled her to pass the drumsticks over to her then-boyfriend/now-husband Zack. (Being Ana is a full-time job.) Now she teaches fourth-grade. She's adorable.

Kate Samworth

Kate was the "fifth Bruiser", there at the get-go, playing sometimes-bass and sometimes-drums back when we were still trying to figure out what the hell was going on. (We're still working on it.) Though she only played one show with us, she made the lasting contribution of giving us our name (a reference to Mary T.'s penchant for affectionately-but-vigorously punching her friends, leaving black-and-blue "love marks").

Zack LeBlanc

Zack has played in a bizillion bands. When Ana relinquished the drum-throne, Zack offered up his well-honed chops. He was our drummer until Katrina, when that wretched storm blew Ana and him westward. He now spends his days in Austin, cursing the mountain-unicyclists and pining for muffulettas. He's adorable.

Peggy O'Neill

After the storm, we were drummerless. But then we found Peggy, darling gal and former drummer for garage rock legends the Gories. Peggy fit right in (though she seemed eternally perplexed by the absence of band-fights). But then she quit smoking and couldn't hang out in bars, which made playing in a bar band sort of untenable. She now plays Gories reunion gigs in big fancy smoke-free venues in big fancy places like New York City.
And so, we quit fighting it. Mary learned the drums, and we've been a trio ever since.
